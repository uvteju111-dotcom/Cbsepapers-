💡 Study Strategy

How to Use PYQs Effectively

Expert tips to get maximum marks using previous year papers.

Tip 01
Solve Under Exam Conditions
Set a 3-hour timer, sit at a desk, and attempt the full paper without any help. This builds the mental stamina and time-sense needed for the actual board exam.
Tip 02
Study the Marking Scheme
After solving, compare with the official marking scheme — not just for correct answers, but to learn how CBSE expects answers to be structured, what key points to include.
Tip 03
Identify Repeating Questions
Many questions appear every 2–3 years. Mark repeating topics across papers — these are your highest-priority areas. SST: Federalism. Science: Electricity, Chemical Reactions.
Tip 04
Start 2 Months Before Boards
Begin solving PYQs after covering 70–80% of the syllabus. Solve 5–7 full papers per subject. This leaves time to plug gaps before the final exam.
Tip 05
Chapter-wise First, Then Full Papers
Start with chapter-wise PYQs to build concept confidence. Then graduate to full-length timed papers. This two-phase approach gives both depth and breadth.
Tip 06
Maps are Free Marks in SST
Map questions in SST are predictable 5-mark opportunities. Practice all standard map items from History and Geography until you can mark them from memory without hesitation.
Tip 07
Review Errors Immediately
Don't wait a week to check your paper. Review mistakes within 24 hours. Keep an error log and revisit it before every subsequent paper you solve.
Tip 08
Check Current Year Pattern First
CBSE updates paper patterns occasionally. Always check the current year's sample paper first before solving older papers to understand the latest format and mark distribution.
Tip 09
Diagrams = Guaranteed Marks in Science
Well-labeled Science diagrams fetch 2–3 marks. Practice human heart, nephron, eye, ray diagrams, and circuit diagrams until you can draw them cleanly from memory.

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I download official CBSE Class 10 PYQs?
+
Download directly from cbse.gov.in. For older archives (2005 onwards), platforms like myCBSEguide, LearnCBSE, Educart, Selfstudys and Vedantu have comprehensive free collections with solutions.
Are PYQs enough to score 90+ in board exams?
+
PYQs are one of the most important tools, but they complement — not replace — NCERT textbooks. Complete NCERT first, then solve 5–7 years of PYQs per subject. CBSE often lifts questions directly from NCERT examples and exercises.
How many years of papers should I solve?
+
Solve at least 5–7 years per subject. Start with 2024 and 2023 (current pattern), then go backwards. Pre-2013 papers are less useful as the pattern has changed significantly.
What is the difference between Delhi and Outside Delhi papers?
+
CBSE conducts exams in multiple zones. Delhi and Outside Delhi papers have similar difficulty levels but some questions differ. Solving both sets gives you broader coverage of possible question types.
Are 2021–22 Term 1 and Term 2 papers useful for current preparation?
+
Term 2 papers (descriptive) are fully useful. Term 1 MCQ papers have limited value for current preparation since CBSE returned to the traditional 80-mark format from 2023 onwards.
What is a Compartment paper and how is it useful?
+
The Compartment Exam is for students who fail in 1–2 subjects. Compartment papers are separate, generally slightly easier, but cover the same syllabus. They make excellent practice material for main exam prep too.
How do I use the marking scheme effectively?
+
After solving a paper, compare each answer with the marking scheme. Note which key value points you missed, what word limits are expected, and how marks are split between points. This teaches you exactly how CBSE wants answers structured.
What is the difference between Computer Applications and Information Technology?
+
Computer Applications (Code 165) focuses on Java/BlueJ programming, HTML, networking and SQL. Information Technology (Code 402) is vocational — covering LibreOffice tools, digital documentation, spreadsheets and databases. The papers are very different. Make sure you're practicing the right one.
When should I start solving PYQs?
+
Start when you've covered 70–80% of the syllabus — ideally 2–3 months before boards. Begin with chapter-wise PYQs, then move to full papers. Consistently solving papers in October–January gives excellent results by February exams.
